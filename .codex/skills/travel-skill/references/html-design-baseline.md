# HTML Design Baseline

## Default Positioning

- High-end travel editorial
- Print-like pacing
- Immersive but restrained
- Designed for sharing, not for dashboard-style operation

## Layout Principles

- Use strong title hierarchy and obvious section entry points
- Preserve generous whitespace so dense travel facts remain readable
- Prefer image-led storytelling and narrative composition over card-first layouts
- Give each section a clear entry, body, and exit rhythm
- Let emphasis come from layout, type, and pacing instead of decorative chrome

## Color and Type

- Avoid generic SaaS palettes and familiar AI-marketing gradients
- Prefer refined, destination-sensitive neutrals with limited accents
- Pair expressive display typography with calm, readable body typography
- Keep contrast strong enough for long-form reading and route comparison

## Content Integrity

- Visual treatment may enrich a section, but may not reorder guide sections
- `recommended`, `comprehensive`, and `daily-overview` must still follow the guide contract
- Desktop and mobile may differ in pacing, not in facts
